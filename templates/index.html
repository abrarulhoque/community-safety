About Community Safety

Community Safety began with a simple idea: what if we could bring together residents, community leaders, and law enforcement in a collaborative platform focused on neighborhood safety?

Our platform provides the tools needed for communities to report incidents, share information securely, and coordinate efforts to create safer neighborhoods. By bringing together various stakeholders, we facilitate more effective communication and response.

We believe that everyone deserves to feel safe in their community, and that safety is best achieved through collaboration, transparency, and shared responsibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our platform, features, and privacy policies.

We take data security seriously. All information is encrypted and stored securely. Personal information is only shared with verified community safety leaders and law enforcement officials. Your camera information, incident reports, and personal details are never sold to third parties. You can adjust your privacy settings at any time in your user profile.

Only verified safety leaders and law enforcement officials can see detailed camera information. Other community members can only see general coverage areas but not specific camera locations or ownership details. We never access your camera feeds directly - if footage is needed for an investigation, a verified official will contact you directly through our secure messaging system.

After submitting a report, it is reviewed by community safety leaders (for non-emergency incidents) or immediately routed to law enforcement (for emergencies). You'll receive a confirmation email with a tracking number and can follow the status of your report on your dashboard. Updates will be provided as the incident is investigated and resolved. For emergencies, always call 911 first, then report the incident on our platform for community tracking.

Community safety leaders are typically appointed by neighborhood associations, HOAs, or existing safety committees. If you're interested in becoming a leader, you can apply through our platform by submitting an application. This requires verification of your identity, address, and role in the community. In some areas, leaders must also complete a basic training course and background check to ensure platform integrity.

The basic platform is free for all community residents. Premium features, such as advanced analytics and community management tools, are available for safety leaders and law enforcement through subscription plans. Many communities fund these subscriptions through HOA or neighborhood association fees. We also offer grant programs for under-resourced communities - contact us to learn more about eligibility.
